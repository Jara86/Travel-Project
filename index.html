Why Edinburgh?

Scotland's enchanting capital

Edinburgh seamlessly blends ancient history with modern charm. From the medieval Old Town to the elegant Georgian New Town, both UNESCO World Heritage sites, the city offers a unique journey through time.

Dominated by its magnificent castle, Edinburgh boasts world-class festivals, cozy pubs, stunning architecture, and centuries of fascinating history. The city's dramatic setting, built among rocky hills and crags, makes it one of Europe's most striking capitals.

Edinburgh's Highlights

Must-see places in Scotland's capital

Edinburgh Castle

Perched atop Castle Rock, this historic fortress dominates the city skyline and offers spectacular views of Edinburgh.

Address:

Castlehill, Edinburgh EH1 2NG

What to see:
  • Crown Jewels of Scotland
  • Stone of Destiny
  • One O'Clock Gun
  • Royal Palace

The Royal Mile

The historic heart of Edinburgh's Old Town, connecting Edinburgh Castle to Holyrood Palace.

Location:

High St, Edinburgh EH1 1PG

What to explore:
  • Historic buildings
  • Street performers
  • Traditional shops
  • Scottish pubs

Arthur's Seat

An ancient volcano offering panoramic views of the city and beyond to the Forth Bridges.

Location:

Queen's Dr, Edinburgh EH8 8HG

What to know:
  • Hiking trails
  • Sunset views
  • Wildlife spotting
  • Photography spots
